Lion’s Mane

Mature sample:

Lion’s Mane mycelium grows in an interesting fluffy, patchy manner. Rhizomorphs spread out and then release clumps of fluffy mycelium which eventually grow together.

This species produces mushroom primordia ( a.k.a. “pins”, which will eventually grow into mature mushrooms ) quite easily on agar.

Phallus Ipidicus

On malt + yeast agar

Inoculated with a piece of agar with live mycelium.

Incubated @ 25 Celsius for 5 Days:

Growth proceeds slowly with this species. Some bacterial contamination was present in all samples. This is what I suspect as the cause of the slow growth. One of the three samples was able to overcome the bacteria and continue growth. I will try to get a clean piece of this sample and transfer it to new agar to observe. The live culture was supplied on a blue agar which has spread its colour into the originally yellow MYA.
